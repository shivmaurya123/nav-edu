1. Reverse osmosis process for water purification The technology of reverse osmosis, sometimes known as RO, is used for water purification. It used a membrane in this technique to break down big molecules since they cannot cross the membrane and remain on the pressured side. The membrane will allow the solvent or clean water to pass through. Molecules of the solute concentrate on one side of the membrane, while the other side of the membrane becomes diluted. The freshwater produced is known as permeate. Wastewater or brine is the concentrated water that remains. Reverse osmosis occurs when the solvent travels across the membrane against the concentration gradient. We know the minimal pressure required to stop solvent flow through a membrane as the osmotic pressure. When higher pressure is supplied to the solvent particles than the osmotic pressure, it moves the solvents from a high concentration zone to a low concentration region, resulting in reverse osmosis. 2.
